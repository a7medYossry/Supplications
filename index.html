Prophetic Supplications for Wudu

Understanding the Sunnah of Du'a during Ablution

1. The Tasmiyah: Saying "Bismillah" at the Start

It is from the Sunnah to begin ablution by mentioning the name of Allah. While scholars have discussed the authenticity level of this specific hadith, it highlights the importance of starting this act of worship with the remembrance of Allah for its completeness and reward.

عَنْ أَبِي هُرَيْرَةَ -رَضِيَ اللَّهُ عَنْهُ- أَنَّ رَسُولَ اللَّهِ صَلَّى اللَّهُ عَلَيْهِ وَسَلَّمَ قَالَ: "لَا وُضُوءَ لِمَنْ لَمْ يَذْكُرِ اسْمَ اللَّهِ عَلَيْهِ"

Narrated by Abu Hurairah (may Allah be pleased with him), the Messenger of Allah (peace and blessings be upon him) said: "There is no ablution for the one who does not mention the name of Allah over it."

2. Supplication During or After Wudu

A beautiful and comprehensive supplication was reported from the Prophet (ﷺ) in the context of his wudu. There is scholarly flexibility on its timing—it can be recited either during the ablution or immediately after its completion. The Prophet's reply, "And have they left anything out?" signifies how comprehensive these words are.

"اللَّهُمَّ اغْفِرْ لِي ذَنْبِي، وَوَسِّعْ لِي فِي دَارِي، وَبَارِكْ لِي فِي رِزْقِي"

"O Allah, forgive my sin, expand my home for me, and bless me in my provision."

3. Supplications After Completing Wudu

It is an emphasized Sunnah to recite specific supplications after finishing the wudu. These narrations contain powerful reminders of Tawhid (monotheism), repentance, and seeking purity.

"أَشْهَدُ أَنْ لَا إِلَهَ إِلَّا اللَّهُ، وَحْدَهُ، لَا شَرِيكَ لَهُ، وَأَشْهَدُ أَنَّ مُحَمَّدًا عَبْدُهُ وَرَسُولُهُ."

"I bear witness that there is no deity worthy of worship except Allah, alone, without a partner, and I bear witness that Muhammad is His servant and Messenger."

(Narrated by Muslim)

An addition to the previous narration is:

"اللَّهُمَّ اجْعَلْنِي مِنَ التَّوَّابِينَ، وَاجْعَلْنِي مِنَ الْمُتَطَهِّرِينَ."

"O Allah, make me among those who repent, and make me among those who purify themselves."

(Narrated by At-Tirmidhi)

"سُبْحَانَكَ اللَّهُمَّ وَبِحَمْدِكَ، أَشْهَدُ أَنْ لَا إِلَهَ إِلَّا أَنْتَ، أَسْتَغْفِرُكَ، وَأَتُوبُ إِلَيْكَ."

"Glory is to You, O Allah, and praise. I bear witness that there is no deity except You. I seek Your forgiveness and I repent to You."

(Narrated by An-Nasa'i)

An Important Note: Supplicating While Washing Each Limb

It is important to clarify that the common practice among some people of reciting a specific supplication for each limb during wudu (e.g., for the face, hands, feet) is not an established practice from the Prophet (ﷺ). Great scholars like Imam an-Nawawi affirmed that there is no authentic narration to support this. Therefore, it is best to adhere to the confirmed Sunnah.

وَقَالَ الْإِمَامُ النَّوَوِيُّ: "وَأَمَّا الدُّعَاءُ عَلَى أَعْضَاءِ الْوُضُوءِ، فَلَمْ يَجِئْ فِيهِ شَيْءٌ عَنِ النَّبِيِّ صَلَّى اللَّهُ عَلَيْهِ وَسَلَّمَ."

Imam an-Nawawi said: "As for the supplication over the limbs of ablution, nothing has been reported in it from the Prophet (ﷺ)."

And Allah knows best.
